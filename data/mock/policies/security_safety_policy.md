## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes procedures for handling security and safety concerns to ensure the protection of guests, staff, and property while maintaining a welcoming environment.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all security and safety incidents including but not limited to:
- Suspicious activity or persons
- Guest safety concerns
- Property security issues
- Emergency situations
- Lost and found items with security implications

---

## 3. General Security Principles

### 3.1 Guest Safety Priority
- Guest safety is the highest priority in all security situations
- All security concerns must be taken seriously and investigated promptly
- Guests should never be made to feel their concerns are dismissed

### 3.2 Confidentiality
- All security incidents must be handled with discretion
- Guest privacy must be maintained unless safety is at risk
- Incident reports are confidential and stored securely

### 3.3 Professional Response
- Security staff must remain calm and professional at all times
- De-escalation techniques should be used whenever possible
- Law enforcement should be contacted when appropriate

---

## 4. Suspicious Activity Procedures

### 4.1 Definition
Suspicious activity includes:
- Unauthorized persons in restricted areas
- Loitering without apparent purpose
- Unusual behavior that raises safety concerns
- Attempts to access guest rooms or property
- Photography or surveillance of security systems

### 4.2 Response Protocol

**Step 1: Initial Assessment (Immediate)**
- Front desk or security staff receives report
- Document: Time, location, description of person/activity
- Assess immediate threat level (Low/Medium/High)

**Step 2: Verification (Within 5 minutes)**
- Review security camera footage if available
- Dispatch security personnel to location
- Maintain visual contact if safe to do so
- Do NOT confront if threat appears high

**Step 3: Action (Within 10 minutes)**

**Low Threat:**
- Security approaches and politely inquires about purpose
- Verify if person is guest or has legitimate business
- Escort to appropriate location or exit if necessary
- Document interaction in incident log

**Medium Threat:**
- Two security personnel approach together
- Maintain safe distance
- Request identification and purpose
- Contact local police for standby if behavior escalates
- Document with photos/video if safe

**High Threat:**
- Immediately contact local police (911)
- Evacuate nearby guests if necessary
- Secure area and maintain visual from safe distance
- Do NOT engage directly
- Activate emergency protocols

**Step 4: Guest Communication (Within 30 minutes)**
- Contact reporting guest to provide update
- Reassure guest of actions taken
- Offer room change if guest feels unsafe
- Provide security escort if requested
- Document guest satisfaction with response

**Step 5: Follow-up (Within 24 hours)**
- Complete detailed incident report
- Review security footage and save relevant clips
- Update security briefing for all shifts
- Assess if additional security measures needed
- Report to management and local authorities if required

## 5. Guest Safety Concerns

### 5.1 Room Security Issues

**Broken Door Locks:**
- Immediate Priority: Move guest to different room
- Timeline: Within 15 minutes
- Maintenance dispatched immediately to repair
- Complimentary room upgrade if available
- Waive room charges for affected night
- Document incident and maintenance completion

**Malfunctioning Room Safe:**
- Assist guest in securing valuables at front desk safe
- Maintenance dispatched within 30 minutes
- Provide temporary lock box if needed
- Repair or replace safe same day
- Compensate guest with points/discount if inconvenienced

**Unauthorized Room Access:**
- Immediately investigate with security
- Review key card access logs
- Check security camera footage
- File police report if theft suspected
- Move guest to new room with new keys
- Offer complimentary stay or significant discount
- Full investigation report within 48 hours

### 5.2 Personal Safety Concerns

**Guest Feels Unsafe:**
- Take concern seriously, never dismiss
- Offer immediate room change to different floor/wing
- Provide security escort to/from room
- Increase security patrols in area
- Provide direct security contact number
- Check in with guest every 4 hours
- Document all actions taken

**Harassment or Stalking:**
- Immediately involve security and management
- Contact local police
- Move guest to secure location (different floor, use service elevator)
- Do not disclose guest room number to anyone
- Flag guest account for extra security
- Provide security escort at all times if requested
- Consider involving hotel legal counsel
- Offer to assist with police report

**Domestic Disturbance:**
- Contact local police immediately
- Ensure safety of all parties
- Separate individuals if safe to do so
- Offer separate rooms to victim
- Provide resources for domestic violence support
- Document incident thoroughly
- Follow up with victim to ensure continued safety

## 6. Fire Safety and Alarms

### 6.1 Fire Alarm Activation

**Immediate Actions:**
- Evacuate building following emergency procedures
- Contact fire department (even if false alarm)
- Account for all guests and staff
- Do not re-enter until cleared by fire department

**Repeated False Alarms:**
- Investigate cause immediately
- Maintenance priority: Fix within 2 hours
- Notify all affected guests
- Offer room change if alarm in specific room
- Provide earplugs and apology amenity
- Discount room rate by 25% for affected night
- If more than 3 false alarms in 24 hours: Evacuate floor and investigate thoroughly

### 6.2 Smoke or Fire Detected

**Priority Actions:**
- Pull fire alarm immediately
- Call 911
- Evacuate building
- Use fire extinguisher only if:
  - Fire is small and contained
  - You have clear exit path
  - You are trained in extinguisher use
- Never risk personal safety
- Account for all guests at assembly point

## 7. Medical Emergencies

### 7.1 Guest Medical Emergency

**Immediate Response:**
- Call 911 for serious medical issues
- Provide first aid if trained and safe to do so
- Do not move injured person unless immediate danger
- Stay with guest until help arrives
- Notify guest's emergency contact if available
- Document incident thoroughly

**Common Situations:**

**Falls/Injuries:**
- Call 911 if serious injury suspected
- Do not move guest
- Provide comfort and reassurance
- Secure area to prevent further incidents
- Take photos of scene (with permission)
- Complete incident report
- Follow up with guest within 24 hours

**Allergic Reactions:**
- Call 911 immediately
- Ask if guest has EpiPen and assist if needed
- Note time of reaction and suspected cause
- Remove allergen source if identified
- Stay with guest until paramedics arrive
- Document all products/foods involved
- Review with housekeeping/kitchen to prevent recurrence

**Medical Equipment Needs:**
- Provide wheelchair if available
- Assist with oxygen tank storage if needed
- Ensure accessible room features working
- Coordinate with medical equipment delivery
- Train staff on any special needs

## 8. Property Security

### 8.1 Lost and Found - High Value Items

**Items Requiring Special Handling:**
- Passports and identification documents
- Credit cards and financial documents
- Jewelry and valuables over $500
- Electronics (laptops, tablets, phones)
- Prescription medications
- Keys (car, home, office)

**Procedure:**
- Log item immediately with detailed description
- Photograph item from multiple angles
- Store in secure safe, not general lost and found
- Attempt to contact guest within 1 hour if recently checked out
- For passports: Contact guest immediately (flight risk)
- Offer to ship overnight at hotel expense for urgent items
- Require ID verification before returning items
- Document chain of custody

**Urgent Passport Recovery:**
- Priority: Contact guest within 15 minutes
- Verify flight time and urgency
- Options:
  1. Guest returns to hotel (provide taxi voucher)
  2. Security delivers to guest location (within 10 miles)
  3. Courier service to airport (hotel pays)
  4. Overnight shipping to guest's destination
- Waive any associated fees
- Provide letter confirming item was left at hotel (for embassy if needed)

### 8.2 Theft Prevention

**Guest Property:**
- Encourage use of room safes
- Provide front desk safe for large valuables
- Security patrols in parking areas
- Well-lit public spaces
- Security cameras in common areas (not in rooms/bathrooms)

**Hotel Property:**
- Inventory control for linens, equipment
- Key card access for restricted areas
- Employee bag checks (if policy allows)
- Regular audits of supplies

## 9. Escalation Matrix

### Immediate (Call 911):
- Active threat to life
- Fire or smoke
- Serious medical emergency
- Violent crime in progress
- Weapons present

### High Priority (Security + Management):
- Guest feels unsafe
- Suspicious activity with medium/high threat
- Property damage
- Harassment or stalking
- Repeated false alarms

### Standard Priority (Security):
- Lost high-value items
- Minor safety concerns
- Noise complaints with safety element
- Low-threat suspicious activity

### Routine (Front Desk):
- General lost and found
- Safety information requests
- Non-urgent maintenance issues

---

## 10. Documentation Requirements

All security incidents must be documented with:
- Date, time, and location
- Names of all involved parties
- Detailed description of incident
- Actions taken and by whom
- Photos/video if applicable
- Witness statements
- Police report number if applicable
- Follow-up actions required
- Resolution and guest satisfaction

**Retention:** Security incident reports retained for 7 years.

---

## 11. Training Requirements

All staff must complete:
- Annual security awareness training
- Emergency evacuation procedures
- Basic first aid and CPR (recommended)
- De-escalation techniques
- Guest communication during incidents

Security staff additional requirements:
- Advanced security training
- Conflict resolution certification
- Local law enforcement liaison
- Quarterly scenario drills

---

## 12. Guest Communication

### During Incident:
- Keep guest informed of actions being taken
- Provide realistic timelines
- Offer alternatives and options
- Show empathy and concern
- Never minimize guest's concerns

### After Resolution:
- Follow up within 24 hours
- Confirm guest satisfaction
- Offer compensation if appropriate
- Thank guest for reporting issue
- Provide direct contact for any further concerns

---

## 13. Compensation Guidelines

**Security-Related Issues:**
- Room change due to safety concern: Complimentary upgrade
- Broken door lock: Waive room charge for affected night
- Repeated false alarms: 25% discount
- Theft from room: Full investigation + compensation based on findings
- Harassment incident: Complimentary stay + points/miles

**Management Approval Required:**
- Compensation over $500
- Complimentary future stays
- Legal liability situations

---

## 14. Legal and Liability

- All incidents with potential liability must be reported to legal counsel
- Preserve all evidence (footage, photos, documents)
- Do not admit fault or liability
- Refer media inquiries to PR department
- Cooperate fully with law enforcement
- Maintain guest confidentiality unless legally required to disclose
