# Accessibility and ADA Compliance Policy – Grand Plaza Hotel

**Last updated:** January 2024  
**Owner:** Guest Services & Facilities Management  
**Document ID:** POL-GS-2024-007

---

## Overview and Commitment

Grand Plaza Hotel is committed to providing accessible accommodations and services to all guests, including those with disabilities. The hotel complies with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and related accessibility regulations, and strives to exceed minimum compliance standards whenever possible. This policy outlines the accessible features available at the property, the procedures for requesting accessible accommodations, and the hotel's approach to ensuring that guests with disabilities can fully enjoy their stay. The hotel recognizes that accessibility needs vary widely among individuals and is committed to working with each guest to identify and provide appropriate accommodations that meet their specific requirements.

---

## Accessible Room Inventory and Features

The hotel maintains a dedicated inventory of accessible guest rooms designed to accommodate guests with various mobility, visual, hearing, and other disabilities. The property has 12 accessible rooms distributed across different room categories, including 8 standard accessible rooms, 3 deluxe accessible rooms, and 1 accessible suite. All accessible rooms are located on floors serviced by elevators and are positioned to minimize travel distance from elevators and other key facilities. The accessible rooms feature wider doorways with a minimum clear width of 32 inches to accommodate wheelchairs and mobility devices, lever-style door handles that can be operated without grasping or twisting, and lowered peepholes and door locks for guests using wheelchairs.

The bathrooms in accessible rooms are designed with roll-in showers or transfer-accessible bathtubs, depending on the specific room configuration. Roll-in showers feature no-threshold entry, built-in or fold-down shower seats, and hand-held shower heads with adjustable height mounting. Transfer-accessible bathtubs include grab bars positioned to assist with safe transfers, and some rooms also feature bathtub lifts upon request. All accessible bathrooms include raised toilet seats with grab bars on both sides, roll-under sinks with lever-style faucets, and adequate clear floor space for wheelchair maneuvering. Bathroom amenities are positioned within reach ranges specified by ADA guidelines, and emergency pull cords are installed near the toilet and in the shower or bathtub area.

In addition to mobility-accessible features, the hotel offers rooms equipped with visual and auditory accessibility features for guests who are deaf, hard of hearing, blind, or have low vision. These rooms include visual notification devices that provide flashing light alerts for the telephone, door knock, doorbell, and fire alarm. Telephone equipment includes amplified handsets and TTY (text telephone) devices, or guests may request video relay service equipment. Alarm clocks with vibrating or flashing alerts are available, and television closed captioning is enabled on all in-room televisions. For guests with visual impairments, the hotel can provide Braille or large-print materials for in-room information, menus, and hotel directories upon request.

## Reservation and Pre-Arrival Process

Guests who require accessible accommodations are encouraged to specify their needs at the time of booking to ensure appropriate room assignment and preparation. The hotel's reservation system includes detailed accessibility codes that allow reservation agents to match guests with rooms that meet their specific requirements. When a guest indicates they need an accessible room, the reservation agent should ask clarifying questions to understand the specific features needed, such as roll-in shower versus transfer tub, visual alert devices, or other specialized equipment. This information is documented in the reservation notes and communicated to the front desk and housekeeping teams prior to the guest's arrival.

For guests booking through online channels, the hotel's website includes detailed descriptions and photographs of accessible room features, allowing guests to review options and select the room type that best meets their needs. The website also provides contact information for guests who have questions about accessibility features or need to discuss specific accommodation requirements that may not be fully addressed through the standard booking process. Third-party booking platforms may have limited accessibility information, so guests booking through OTAs or travel agents are encouraged to contact the hotel directly after booking to confirm that their accessibility needs will be met.

Pre-arrival communication is particularly important for guests with accessibility needs. The hotel sends a pre-arrival email to all guests with accessible room reservations, confirming the specific features of their assigned room and inviting them to contact the hotel if they have additional questions or requirements. This email also provides information about accessible parking, accessible entrances, and other property features that may be relevant to their stay. For guests who have indicated they will be traveling with service animals, the pre-arrival communication includes information about the hotel's service animal policy and designated relief areas on the property.

## Check-In Process and Room Assignment

Upon arrival, guests with accessible room reservations receive priority check-in assistance. Front desk agents are trained to greet these guests warmly and efficiently, recognizing that some guests may have been traveling for extended periods and may be fatigued. The check-in counter includes a lowered section to accommodate guests using wheelchairs, and agents are instructed to move to this section when assisting guests who would benefit from the lower counter height. During check-in, the agent should confirm the guest's accessibility requirements and verify that the assigned room meets those needs. If the guest indicates that additional accommodations or equipment are needed, the agent should coordinate with the appropriate departments to fulfill these requests promptly.

Room assignment for accessible rooms follows specific protocols to ensure that these rooms are reserved for guests who genuinely need them. The hotel does not assign accessible rooms to guests who have not requested accessibility features unless all other room types are sold out, and even in those circumstances, accessible rooms are the last inventory to be assigned to non-disabled guests. This practice ensures that accessible rooms remain available for guests who need them, even during high occupancy periods. If a situation arises where an accessible room must be assigned to a guest without accessibility needs due to extreme overbooking, that room is flagged as the first to be moved if a guest with accessibility needs arrives and requires that specific room type.

The front desk agent should provide the guest with a brief orientation to the accessible features of their room and offer to have a staff member escort them to the room to provide a more detailed orientation if desired. This is particularly helpful for guests with visual impairments who may benefit from a guided tour of the room layout and location of amenities. The agent should also inform the guest about accessible routes to key hotel facilities such as restaurants, meeting rooms, fitness center, and pool, and provide information about accessible transportation options if the guest plans to explore the local area.

## Service Animal Policy

Grand Plaza Hotel welcomes service animals and is committed to accommodating guests who rely on service animals for assistance. Under ADA regulations, a service animal is defined as a dog that has been individually trained to perform tasks or do work for a person with a disability. The hotel may ask two questions to determine if an animal qualifies as a service animal: whether the animal is required because of a disability, and what work or task the animal has been trained to perform. The hotel cannot require documentation of the animal's training or certification, cannot require the animal to demonstrate its task, and cannot inquire about the nature of the guest's disability.

Service animals are permitted in all areas of the hotel where guests are normally allowed, including guest rooms, restaurants, meeting spaces, and public areas. The only exceptions are areas where health codes prohibit animals, such as commercial kitchens, although service animals are permitted in dining areas where food is served to guests. The hotel does not charge pet fees or deposits for service animals, as they are not considered pets under ADA regulations. However, guests are responsible for any damage caused by their service animal, and standard hotel policies regarding guest responsibility for property damage apply.

The hotel provides designated relief areas for service animals on the property grounds. These areas are clearly marked and include waste disposal stations with bags and receptacles. The locations of relief areas are communicated to guests with service animals at check-in, and maps showing these locations are available at the front desk. Housekeeping staff are trained to clean rooms occupied by guests with service animals using the same standards as all other rooms, and no special cleaning protocols or fees apply unless there is unusual damage or excessive pet hair requiring additional cleaning time.

In rare cases where a service animal's behavior poses a direct threat to the health or safety of others, or if the animal is not housebroken or is out of control and the handler does not take effective action to control it, the hotel may ask that the animal be removed from the premises. However, the guest must still be offered the opportunity to stay at the hotel without the animal. Such situations are extremely rare and are handled by management with careful consideration of ADA requirements and the guest's rights.

## Accessible Public Spaces and Facilities

Beyond accessible guest rooms, the hotel has designed its public spaces and facilities to be accessible to all guests. The main entrance features an accessible ramp with appropriate slope and handrails, and automatic doors that can be activated by push buttons positioned at accessible heights. The lobby includes accessible seating areas with clear floor space for wheelchair users, and the front desk includes the previously mentioned lowered counter section. Accessible restrooms are located in the lobby area and near all major public spaces including restaurants, meeting rooms, and recreational facilities.

The hotel's restaurant and bar areas include accessible seating options with tables at appropriate heights and adequate clear floor space for wheelchair users. Menus are available in large print and Braille upon request, and restaurant staff are trained to provide assistance to guests with disabilities as needed, such as reading menu items aloud or providing detailed descriptions of dishes. The hotel's meeting and event spaces are designed to accommodate attendees with disabilities, with accessible seating areas, assistive listening systems available upon request, and accessible podiums and presentation areas for speakers with disabilities.

Recreational facilities including the fitness center and swimming pool are designed with accessibility in mind. The fitness center includes equipment that can be used by guests with various disabilities, and staff can provide orientation to accessible equipment features. The pool area includes a pool lift that allows guests with mobility impairments to enter and exit the pool safely, and pool staff are trained in the operation of the lift and can provide assistance as needed. Accessible changing rooms and restrooms are located adjacent to the pool area. The hotel's spa, if available, includes accessible treatment rooms and can accommodate guests with disabilities for most services, with advance notice allowing staff to prepare appropriately.

## Assistive Equipment and Services

In addition to the permanent accessibility features built into the property, the hotel maintains an inventory of assistive equipment and devices that can be provided to guests upon request. This equipment includes shower chairs and bathtub benches for guests who need additional support in the bathroom, raised toilet seats for guests who require higher seating, grab bars that can be temporarily installed in rooms that don't have permanent grab bars, bed rails for guests who need assistance with bed transfers, and refrigerators for guests who need to store medication.

For guests with hearing impairments, the hotel can provide amplified telephones, TTY devices, visual alert systems, and vibrating alarm clocks. For guests with visual impairments, the hotel can provide large-button telephones, Braille or large-print materials, and magnifying glasses. The hotel also maintains relationships with local vendors who can provide specialized equipment on short notice if a guest requires something not in the hotel's standard inventory. Requests for assistive equipment should be made at the time of booking when possible, but the hotel will make every effort to fulfill requests made at check-in or during the stay, recognizing that guests' needs may change or may not be fully anticipated in advance.

The hotel can arrange for sign language interpreters or other communication assistance services for guests who are deaf or hard of hearing, particularly for important communications such as emergency information, billing discussions, or coordination of special services. While the hotel requests advance notice for interpreter services to ensure availability, staff will make every effort to arrange services even for last-minute requests. The cost of interpreter services for routine hotel communications is borne by the hotel as a reasonable accommodation under ADA requirements.

## Staff Training and Awareness

All hotel staff members receive training on disability awareness, ADA compliance, and appropriate interaction with guests with disabilities. This training emphasizes treating all guests with respect and dignity, offering assistance without being presumptuous, and understanding that guests with disabilities are the best judges of what assistance they need. Staff are trained to speak directly to guests with disabilities rather than to companions or interpreters, to avoid making assumptions about guests' capabilities based on visible disabilities, and to ask before providing assistance, as unsolicited help can sometimes be unwelcome or even dangerous if it interferes with a guest's use of mobility devices or service animals.

Front desk staff receive specialized training on accessible room features, assistive equipment inventory, and procedures for coordinating accessibility-related requests with other departments. Housekeeping staff are trained on cleaning and maintaining accessible rooms, including proper positioning of grab bars, shower seats, and other accessibility features, and are instructed never to move or remove accessibility equipment from rooms. Maintenance staff receive training on ADA compliance standards for facilities and are responsible for conducting regular inspections of accessible features to ensure they remain in good working order.

The hotel designates an ADA Coordinator who serves as the primary point of contact for accessibility-related questions, concerns, and complaints. The ADA Coordinator is responsible for ensuring ongoing compliance with accessibility regulations, coordinating staff training, managing the assistive equipment inventory, and serving as a resource for staff members who encounter situations requiring accessibility accommodations. The ADA Coordinator's contact information is available at the front desk and on the hotel's website.

## Emergency Evacuation Procedures

The hotel has developed specific emergency evacuation procedures for guests with disabilities to ensure their safety in the event of fire, natural disaster, or other emergency requiring building evacuation. Guests with mobility impairments who are staying in rooms above the ground floor are provided with information about areas of refuge, which are fire-rated areas near stairwells where guests can wait safely for assistance from emergency responders if elevators are not operational. The hotel's emergency response team is trained to prioritize assistance to guests with disabilities during evacuations, and staff conduct regular drills that include scenarios involving guests with various types of disabilities.

Guests with disabilities are encouraged to inform the front desk of any special assistance they may need during an emergency evacuation. This information is documented in the reservation and communicated to the hotel's security and emergency response teams. For guests who are deaf or hard of hearing, the hotel's fire alarm system includes visual alert devices in accessible rooms, and staff are trained to provide visual communication during emergencies. For guests with visual impairments, staff are trained to provide clear verbal instructions and physical guidance during evacuations if needed.

The hotel maintains evacuation chairs in stairwells that can be used to safely transport guests with mobility impairments down stairs if elevators are not operational. Staff members are trained in the use of these devices, and their locations are clearly marked. The hotel's emergency procedures are reviewed and updated annually, and accessibility considerations are a key component of these reviews.

## Feedback and Continuous Improvement

The hotel actively seeks feedback from guests with disabilities to identify opportunities for improving accessibility features and services. Guest satisfaction surveys include specific questions about accessibility, and guests are encouraged to provide detailed feedback about their experiences. The hotel's management team reviews this feedback regularly and uses it to inform decisions about property improvements, staff training enhancements, and policy updates. When guests identify accessibility barriers or suggest improvements, the hotel investigates these concerns promptly and implements changes when feasible.

The hotel conducts periodic accessibility audits using both internal assessments and external consultants with expertise in ADA compliance and universal design. These audits identify areas where the property exceeds accessibility standards as well as areas where improvements may be needed. The hotel maintains a capital improvement plan that prioritizes accessibility enhancements, and budget is allocated annually for accessibility-related improvements even when not required by regulation changes.
