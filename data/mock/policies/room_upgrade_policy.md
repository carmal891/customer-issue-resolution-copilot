# Room Upgrade Policy – Grand Plaza Hotel

**Last updated:** February 2024  
**Owner:** Front Office & Guest Services  
**Document ID:** POL-FO-2024-003

---

## Purpose

This document establishes the guidelines and procedures for room upgrades at Grand Plaza Hotel. The policy aims to maximize guest satisfaction while optimizing revenue and maintaining operational efficiency.

Room upgrades can occur through several channels: complimentary upgrades based on availability and guest status, paid upgrades requested by guests, and operational upgrades necessitated by hotel circumstances.

---

## Complimentary Upgrade Eligibility

Complimentary room upgrades are offered at the hotel's discretion based on availability, guest loyalty status, and operational considerations. These upgrades are never guaranteed and should not be promised during the booking process.

### Loyalty Program Priority

The hotel's loyalty program members receive priority consideration for complimentary upgrades. Diamond Status members who have stayed 75 or more nights annually receive first priority for available upgrades. Platinum Status members with 50 or more nights annually receive second priority, followed by Gold Status members with 25 or more nights annually.

When multiple guests of the same status level are eligible for upgrades on the same day, priority is determined by check-in time, with earlier arrivals receiving preference. In cases where check-in times are similar, the guest with the longer length of stay receives priority.

It is important to note that loyalty status alone does not guarantee an upgrade. Availability of higher room categories is the primary determining factor. During periods of high occupancy, even Diamond members may not receive upgrades if no higher-category rooms are available.

### Special Occasions

Guests celebrating special occasions such as honeymoons, anniversaries, or significant birthdays may be considered for complimentary upgrades when availability permits. These occasions should be noted in the reservation at the time of booking or communicated to the front desk prior to arrival.

Front desk staff have discretion to offer upgrades for special occasions, but should verify availability and consider current occupancy levels before making commitments. During peak periods, special occasion upgrades may not be feasible.

### Service Recovery

Complimentary upgrades may be offered as a service recovery gesture when guests experience issues during their stay. Examples include room maintenance problems, noise disturbances, or other service failures that impact the guest experience.

Service recovery upgrades should be approved by the Front Office Manager or Manager on Duty. The decision should consider the severity of the issue, the guest's history with the hotel, and the availability of alternative rooms.

## Paid Upgrade Options

Guests may request paid upgrades at any time from booking through check-in. Paid upgrades are subject to availability and pricing varies based on demand, season, and the specific room categories involved.

### Upgrade Pricing Structure

Upgrade pricing is dynamic and calculated based on the difference between the booked room category and the desired upgrade category. During periods of high demand, upgrade prices may be higher than during low-demand periods.

As a general guideline, upgrades from Standard rooms to Deluxe rooms typically range from $30 to $50 per night. Upgrades from Standard or Deluxe rooms to Junior Suites typically range from $60 to $100 per night. Upgrades to full Suites vary significantly based on the specific suite type and availability, generally ranging from $150 to $300 per night.

These prices are guidelines only and may be adjusted based on current demand and inventory levels. Front desk staff should check the current upgrade pricing in the property management system before quoting prices to guests.

### Advance Upgrade Requests

Guests may request paid upgrades at the time of booking or any time prior to arrival. Advance upgrade requests are subject to availability and the quoted price may change if the request is made closer to the arrival date.

When a guest requests an upgrade in advance, the reservation should be modified to reflect the new room category and the price difference should be collected or added to the reservation balance. A new confirmation email should be sent reflecting the upgraded room category.

### At Check-In Upgrades

Many guests inquire about upgrade options at the time of check-in. Front desk staff should be prepared to present available upgrade options with current pricing.

The upgrade presentation should be handled professionally and without pressure. Staff should briefly describe the benefits of available upgrade categories and provide pricing, then allow the guest to make their decision. If a guest declines an upgrade offer, the interaction should end gracefully without further persuasion.

## Operational Upgrades

Operational upgrades occur when the hotel must move a guest to a different room category due to circumstances beyond the guest's control. These situations require careful handling to maintain guest satisfaction.

### Overbooking Situations

In rare cases where the hotel has overbooked a particular room category, guests may need to be upgraded to a higher category at no additional charge. This is referred to as a "walk-up" upgrade.

When overbooking necessitates upgrades, the Front Office Manager should identify which guests will receive upgrades based on several factors. Guests with shorter stays are typically upgraded first, as they occupy the higher category for fewer nights. Guests without loyalty status may be prioritized over loyalty members, who may prefer to maintain their booked category for points purposes. Guests who booked non-refundable rates may be prioritized as they have less flexibility.

The upgrade should be presented to the guest as a positive enhancement rather than as a problem resolution. Staff should emphasize the benefits of the higher room category and ensure the guest understands there is no additional charge.

### Room Maintenance Issues

When a guest's assigned room becomes unavailable due to maintenance issues discovered after check-in, the guest must be moved to an alternative room. If a comparable room in the same category is not available, an upgrade to a higher category should be offered at no charge.

In these situations, the hotel should also consider offering additional compensation such as complimentary breakfast, spa credits, or other amenities to acknowledge the inconvenience of changing rooms.

### Room Type Mismatches

Occasionally, a guest may arrive to find that their assigned room does not match their expectations based on the booking description. This can occur due to miscommunication, booking errors, or misunderstanding of room category descriptions.

When a room type mismatch occurs, front desk staff should first attempt to clarify the guest's expectations and explain the features of their booked room category. If the guest remains dissatisfied and a higher category room is available, an upgrade may be offered. The decision to charge for the upgrade or offer it complimentarily should be made by the Front Office Manager based on the circumstances.

## Upgrade Request Procedures

### Guest-Initiated Requests

When a guest requests an upgrade, front desk staff should follow a standard procedure to ensure consistency and professionalism.

First, verify the guest's current room category and reservation details in the property management system. Check availability of higher room categories for the guest's entire length of stay. Review the guest's loyalty status and stay history. Calculate the upgrade price based on current rates and the specific room categories involved.

Present the available options to the guest clearly, describing the features and benefits of each higher category. Provide pricing for each option. Allow the guest time to consider without applying pressure. If the guest accepts, process the upgrade in the system, collect payment if applicable, and issue a new room key and confirmation.

### Staff-Initiated Offers

Front desk staff may proactively offer upgrades to guests during check-in when higher category rooms are available and occupancy levels permit. This is particularly appropriate for loyalty program members, guests celebrating special occasions, or guests who have experienced previous service issues.

When offering an upgrade, staff should be clear about whether the upgrade is complimentary or paid. The offer should be framed as an opportunity to enhance the guest's experience rather than as a sales pitch.

## Loyalty Program Upgrade Benefits

### Confirmed Suite Upgrades

Diamond Status members are eligible for confirmed suite upgrades when booking specific rate plans. This benefit guarantees a suite upgrade, subject to availability at check-in, when the member books at least 5 days in advance.

The confirmed suite upgrade benefit applies to the member's room only and does not extend to additional rooms in the same reservation. If suite inventory is exhausted at check-in despite the confirmed upgrade benefit, the hotel must provide alternative compensation such as a significant room rate discount or complimentary amenities.

### Upgrade Certificates

Platinum and Diamond members receive annual upgrade certificates that can be applied to reservations. Platinum members receive two certificates annually, while Diamond members receive four certificates annually.

Upgrade certificates can be applied at the time of booking or up to 48 hours before arrival, subject to availability. Each certificate provides a one-category upgrade for up to three consecutive nights. Certificates cannot be combined to achieve multi-category upgrades.

Certificates expire 12 months from the date of issuance and cannot be extended or transferred to other members.

## Suite Upgrade Considerations

Suites represent the hotel's premium inventory and upgrades to suite categories require additional considerations beyond standard room upgrades.

### Suite Availability Management

Suite inventory is carefully managed to balance upgrade opportunities with direct suite bookings. The Front Office Manager monitors suite availability daily and provides guidance to front desk staff regarding suite upgrade authorization.

During periods of high suite demand, complimentary suite upgrades may be restricted even for Diamond members. Paid suite upgrades remain available subject to inventory.

### Suite Amenities and Services

Guests upgraded to suites should be informed of the additional amenities and services available to suite guests. This may include access to the executive lounge, complimentary breakfast, evening reception, and enhanced in-room amenities.

Front desk staff should provide suite guests with information about these benefits at check-in to ensure they take full advantage of the suite experience.

## Upgrade Restrictions and Limitations

Certain situations limit or prevent room upgrades, and staff should be aware of these restrictions to avoid guest disappointment.

### Group Bookings

Individual rooms within group blocks are generally not eligible for complimentary upgrades, as group rates are negotiated based on specific room categories. Paid upgrades may be available for group attendees, but should be coordinated with the group organizer and the hotel's group sales team.

### Third-Party Bookings

Reservations made through online travel agencies or other third-party channels have limited upgrade flexibility. Complimentary upgrades may still be offered at check-in based on availability, but paid upgrades typically cannot be processed as the OTA is the merchant of record.

Guests who wish to purchase an upgrade for an OTA booking should be directed to contact the OTA directly or, alternatively, the hotel may offer to cancel the OTA booking and create a new direct booking with the upgraded room category, subject to rate availability and cancellation policies.

### Non-Refundable Rates

Guests who booked non-refundable rates are eligible for both complimentary and paid upgrades. However, if a paid upgrade is purchased, the upgrade fee itself is also non-refundable, consistent with the original booking terms.

## Communication and Guest Expectations

Clear communication about upgrade policies and availability is essential to managing guest expectations and avoiding disappointment.

### At Booking

Reservation agents should not promise or guarantee upgrades during the booking process unless the guest is purchasing a confirmed upgrade or using an upgrade certificate. Phrases like "we'll see what we can do" or "upgrades are often available" should be avoided as they create expectations that may not be met.

### Pre-Arrival

Pre-arrival communications should not reference potential upgrades unless the guest has specifically purchased an upgrade or is using a confirmed upgrade benefit. Unsolicited mentions of upgrades in pre-arrival emails can create expectations that lead to disappointment if upgrades are not available at check-in.

### At Check-In

Front desk staff should handle upgrade discussions professionally and efficiently. If offering an upgrade, clearly state whether it is complimentary or paid, and provide specific pricing if applicable. If a guest inquires about upgrades but none are available, staff should politely explain that all higher categories are fully occupied and offer to note the guest's interest for potential future availability during their stay.

## Revenue Management Considerations

Upgrade decisions should balance guest satisfaction with revenue optimization. The Revenue Management team provides guidance on upgrade strategies based on current and forecasted demand.

During periods of low occupancy in higher room categories, complimentary upgrades may be encouraged to enhance guest satisfaction without significant revenue impact. During periods of high demand, paid upgrades should be emphasized to capture additional revenue.

Front desk staff should consult with the Front Office Manager or Manager on Duty when uncertain about upgrade decisions, particularly for high-value upgrades or during peak demand periods.

---

## Documentation and Tracking

All upgrades, whether complimentary or paid, must be properly documented in the property management system. This documentation is essential for revenue tracking, guest history, and operational analysis.

When processing an upgrade, staff should note the reason for the upgrade in the reservation notes. Reasons may include loyalty status, paid upgrade, service recovery, operational necessity, or special occasion. For complimentary upgrades, the authorization source such as Front Office Manager or Manager on Duty should be documented.

Upgrade tracking data is reviewed monthly by the Front Office Manager and Revenue Manager to analyze upgrade patterns, revenue impact, and guest satisfaction outcomes.

---

## Staff Training and Authority Levels

Front desk agents are authorized to offer complimentary upgrades of one category, such as Standard to Deluxe, without manager approval when availability permits and the guest meets eligibility criteria such as loyalty status or special occasion.

Multi-category complimentary upgrades, such as Standard to Junior Suite, require Front Office Manager or Manager on Duty approval. Suite upgrades require Front Office Manager approval except for Diamond members with confirmed suite upgrade benefits.

All staff members should receive regular training on upgrade policies, procedures, and current pricing. Training should emphasize the importance of managing guest expectations and maintaining consistency in upgrade decisions.

---

## Contact and Escalation

For questions about upgrade policies or specific upgrade situations, front desk staff should contact the Front Office Manager during business hours, the Manager on Duty after hours and weekends, or the Revenue Manager for pricing and availability questions.

Guest complaints or disputes regarding upgrades should be escalated to the Front Office Manager or Manager on Duty immediately.

---

## Policy Review and Updates

This policy is reviewed quarterly by the Front Office Manager and Revenue Manager to ensure alignment with current market conditions, competitive positioning, and guest satisfaction goals.

Updates to upgrade pricing, eligibility criteria, or procedures are communicated to all front desk staff through team meetings and updated training materials.
